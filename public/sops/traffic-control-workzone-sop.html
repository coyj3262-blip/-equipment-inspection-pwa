Traffic Control and Work Zone Management Standard Operating Procedure

Weekly Acknowledgment: Required for traffic control supervisors, flaggers, spotters, and any employee working inside an active road or highway work zone.

Scope: Applies to setup, maintenance, and removal of temporary traffic control for lane closures, detours, flagging operations, and mobile work zones on public roadways.

1. Purpose

Protect workers and road users by ensuring traffic control devices are installed and maintained per MUTCD and state DOT requirements while providing safe ingress and egress for construction activities.

2. Roles and Responsibilities

Role Key Duties
Traffic Control Supervisor (TCS) Develop traffic control plans, conduct pre-shift inspections, communicate with owner or agency, maintain device inventory.
Flaggers and Spotters Maintain visual contact, use standardized hand signals or STOP-SLOW paddles, protect work area during equipment moves.
Project Supervisor Coordinate work sequencing with TCS, ensure crew compliance with plan, report incidents.
All Personnel Wear required high-visibility PPE, stay within protected zone, maintain situational awareness.

3. Pre-Deployment Checklist

  • Review approved traffic control plan (TCP) and any agency permits or restrictions.
  • Check weather, sight distance, and existing roadway conditions.
  • Verify quantity and condition of signs, cones, drums, Type III barricades, arrow boards, and lighting.
  • Ensure communication devices (radios) charged and on agreed channel.
  • Conduct job briefing covering set-up sequence, emergency plans, and roadway speed limits.

4. Required PPE

  • ANSI 107 Class 2 vest minimum for daytime; Class 3 for night or speeds at or above 45 mph.
  • Hard hat with reflective striping and chin strap where wind exposure exists.
  • Steel-toe boots with slip-resistant soles; gloves suitable for handling devices.
  • Two-way radio with shoulder microphone for all flaggers and TCS.
  • Flashlights or illuminated batons for night operations.

5. Setup Procedure

  1. Stage advance warning signs in order from farthest upstream to the work area, maintaining taper lengths per MUTCD table.
  2. Deploy channelizing devices to form tapers, buffer space, and work zone; maintain maximum spacing guidelines.
  3. Position arrow boards and portable changeable message signs as indicated on TCP.
  4. Install temporary rumble strips, lighting, or portable traffic signals where required.
  5. Confirm ingress and egress points for construction vehicles are protected by flaggers or temporary signals.

6. Work Zone Operations

  • Maintain constant communication between flaggers; use radios and eye contact before releasing traffic.
  • Inspect traffic control devices twice per shift and after weather events or traffic impacts.
  • Remove or cover signs not applicable to current operation.
  • Ensure work vehicles enter or exit with escort; never back into live traffic without a spotter.
  • Report near misses, intrusions, or citizen complaints immediately to TCS and supervisor.

7. Demobilization

  1. Reverse the setup order: remove devices from the work area back to the advance warning area.
  2. Ensure roadway is free of debris, temporary striping residue, or equipment prior to reopening.
  3. Return devices to storage, inspect for damage, and document replacements needed.
  4. Complete post-operation report noting traffic volumes, incidents, and improvement ideas.

8. Weekly Acknowledgment and Documentation

  • TCS verifies all personnel assigned to traffic control have acknowledged this SOP within the past 7 days.
  • Maintain daily traffic control log with weather, inspections, and device counts.
  • Submit incident and intrusion reports to safety department within 8 hours.
