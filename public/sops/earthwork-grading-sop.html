Earthwork and Rough Grading Standard Operating Procedure

Weekly Acknowledgment: Required for dozer and loader operators, grade-checkers, and survey technicians prior to earthwork assignments.

Daily Setup Checklist

  • Review survey control, design surfaces, and daily cut and fill targets.
  • Verify equipment telematics, GPS systems, and machine control files are updated.
  • Inspect haul routes, dump sites, and erosion control devices.
  • Complete JSA and stretching session before mounting equipment.

1. Purpose

Ensure earthwork activities achieve design elevations and compaction requirements while preventing rollover incidents, underground utility strikes, and environmental violations.

2. Equipment and Personnel

Equipment Primary Hazards Control Measures
Dozers with GPS control Rollover on slopes, striking underground utilities Maintain slope charts, stay within design slope limits, use spotter near utilities
Wheel and track loaders Tip-over on soft ground, collision with trucks Perform load counts, use designated dump zones, maintain three points of contact
Compaction equipment Vibration exposure, rollovers Limit exposure intervals, use proper drum type, avoid cross-slopes steeper than 5:1

3. Required PPE

  • Hard hat, safety glasses, high-visibility apparel Class 2 or better, gloves suited to task.
  • Hearing protection when operating or working within 25 feet of running heavy equipment.
  • Steel-toe boots with slip-resistant soles; fall protection harness if working near ponds or steep slopes.

4. Procedure

  1. Planning: Supervisor issues daily earthwork plan with target grades, haul routes, stockpile locations, and stormwater controls.
  2. Pre-Operational Inspection: Operators inspect machines (fluids, belts, alarms, seatbelts) and ensure ROPS/FOPS in place.
  3. Grade Establishment: Survey crew sets control stakes, benchmarks, and verifies model alignment on machine control.
  4. Cut and Fill Execution: Remove topsoil as required, maintain minimum 2% surface drainage, and avoid over-excavation beyond tolerance.
  5. Compaction: Place fill in maximum 8-inch lifts unless otherwise specified, moisture condition the soil, and compact to required density. Record roller pass counts.
  6. Erosion Control: Stabilize slopes with matting or temporary seeding at end of shift; install silt fence or wattles as indicated on plan.
  7. Verification: Grade-checker or surveyor spot-check elevations every 50 feet or as needed and document results in daily log.
  8. Wrap-Up: Clean equipment, fuel in designated area with spill containment, and update daily production and hauling log.

5. Quality and Safety Controls

  • Flag or barricade over-excavated areas until backfilled.
  • Never push material closer than 5 feet to excavation edges without shoring approval.
  • Keep haul roads watered but not slick; use dust control agents as required by environmental permits.
  • Report any grade deviation greater than 0.10 feet immediately for redesign or rework.
  • Supervisor conducts weekly slope and bench inspections with the competent person.

6. Documentation

  • Daily earthwork production log (volume moved, trucks per hour, compaction results).
  • Weekly acknowledgment roster stored in project quality folder.
  • Stormwater inspection reports and erosion control maintenance logs.

7. References

  • Project grading plan sheets and digital terrain model.
  • OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart O — Motor Vehicles, Mechanized Equipment, and Marine Operations.
  • State stormwater permit conditions and erosion control manual.
