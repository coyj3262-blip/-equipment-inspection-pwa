Concrete Placement and Finishing Standard Operating Procedure

Weekly Acknowledgment: Required for placement crews, pump operators, finishers, and quality control technicians.

Scope: Applies to all ready-mix concrete placements, including slabs-on-grade, structural footings, walls, pavements, and flatwork performed by company crews.

1. Purpose

Deliver concrete placements that meet project specifications for strength, finish, and durability while controlling hydration, preventing mix contamination, and maintaining worker safety.

2. Pre-Pour Planning

  1. Review contract specifications: mix design, slump range, entrained air, reinforcement placement, tolerances, curing requirements.
  2. Verify formwork, reinforcement, embeds, and sleeves have passed inspection; ensure release agent applied.
  3. Confirm delivery sequence with batch plant (truck spacing, total yardage) and pump availability.
  4. Prepare contingency plan for weather (wind, temperature, precipitation) including accelerators, retarders, blankets, or wind breaks.
  5. Assign finishing equipment, screeds, bull floats, power trowels, and curing compounds; verify operational readiness.

3. Personnel and PPE

Role Key Duties Required PPE
Pour Supervisor Coordinate delivery, quality checks, communications with inspector Hard hat, safety glasses, high-visibility vest, waterproof boots
Placement Crew Direct pump discharge, consolidate concrete, maintain tolerances Cut-resistant gloves, rubber boots, eye protection, hearing protection near vibrators
Finishers Float, trowel, edge, joint, and surface finish to specified texture Knee pads, appropriate footwear, respirator when saw-cutting control joints
QC Technician Conduct slump, temperature, air content tests, and make cylinders Lab coat or apron, safety glasses with side shields, rubber gloves

4. Placement Procedure

  1. Arrival and Inspection: Inspect truck ticket; verify mix design, batch time, and water added. Reject loads exceeding time limits without approval.
  2. Testing: Perform slump, temperature, and air content tests on first truck and every 50 cubic yards or per specification. Cast cylinders as required.
  3. Discharge: Direct pump or chute to avoid segregation, place concrete in layers not exceeding 18 inches, and maintain uniform flow.
  4. Consolidation: Use internal vibrators at one-foot spacing, insert vertically, and withdraw slowly to prevent voids. Avoid over-vibration.
  5. Strike-Off: Use screed to achieve grades; check with laser or level and adjust immediately.
  6. Floating and Finishing: Bull float immediately after strike-off. Begin power trowel passes after bleed water dissipates. Follow specified finish (broom, trowel, exposed aggregate).
  7. Jointing: Install control joints at planned locations within required time window using saws or hand tools as specified.
  8. Curing: Apply curing compound or use wet curing and blankets within 30 minutes of final finish. Protect surface from rapid moisture loss.
  9. Cold or Hot Weather Measures: Maintain concrete between 50°F and 70°F during curing in cold weather; use insulation blankets as needed. For hot weather, cool mix, shade placements, and use set retarders.

5. Safety Considerations

  • Maintain exclusion zone around pump boom and outriggers; inspect for overhead power lines (minimum clearance 20 feet).
  • Use spotters to direct trucks backing into placement area; ensure wheel chocks on slopes.
  • Manage washout water in containment basins; never discharge to storm drains.
  • Monitor crew for signs of heat stress or cold stress; rotate tasks and provide hydration or rest breaks.
  • Ensure silica dust controls when saw-cutting joints, with preference for wet cutting.

6. Quality Verification

  • Document all test results, batch ticket adjustments, and weather data on pour log.
  • Verify reinforcement cover and embed placement prior to pour using checklists.
  • Inspect finished surface with straightedge; identify high or low spots and correct immediately.
  • Record curing start and end times and methods used.

7. Weekly Acknowledgment and Documentation

  • Supervisors audit acknowledgment status before scheduling personnel on concrete pours.
  • Retain pour logs, test reports, and QA documentation with project records.
  • Log any non-conformances, corrective actions, and lessons learned for review at weekly coordination meeting.
