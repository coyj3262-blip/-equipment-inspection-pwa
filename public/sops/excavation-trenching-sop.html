Excavation and Trenching Standard Operating Procedure

Weekly Review Required: Every crewmember working in or around trenches must read and acknowledge this SOP once every 7 calendar days.

Scope: Applies to all open-cut excavation, trenching for utilities, and pit excavations deeper than 4 feet on civil construction projects.

1. Purpose

Provide a consistent process that prevents cave-ins, struck-by incidents, and underground utility strikes while maintaining production goals for trenching operations.

2. Roles and Responsibilities

Role Responsibilities
Competent Person Conduct soil classification, daily inspections, protective system selection, atmospheric testing in confined trenches, and stop work for unsafe conditions.
Excavator Operator Follow dig plan, maintain slope or benching, keep spoil pile 2 feet from edge, coordinate with spotter, and maintain clear swing radius.
Spotter or Labor Crew Maintain clear communication, install trench boxes or shoring, monitor water accumulation, and ensure access ladders are within 25 feet.
Project Supervisor Verify utility locates, approve protective system design, ensure competent person is assigned, and audit weekly acknowledgments.

3. Pre-Task Requirements

  • Call for utility locates (811 or state system) at least 72 hours prior; mark locates on the ground and on plan set.
  • Review Job Safety Analysis (JSA) daily and confirm all crew members sign prior to starting work.
  • Inspect excavation equipment (excavators, loaders) for leaks, proper functioning, and safety devices.
  • Confirm trench protective system (sloping, benching, shield, shoring) is available and rated for depth and soil type.
  • Stage dewatering pumps if water is present or forecasted; ensure discharge lines are directed away from excavation.

4. Required PPE and Tools

  • Hard hat, safety glasses with side shields, high-visibility vest, and Grade 1 protective footwear.
  • Cut-resistant gloves, hearing protection for work near running equipment, and respiratory protection if required by atmospheric testing.
  • Gas monitor with calibration record when entering trenches 4 feet or deeper that could accumulate hazardous atmospheres.
  • Ladders or engineered stair systems extending 3 feet above trench edge for access and egress.

5. Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Daily Briefing: Conduct toolbox talk, review JSA, utility map, and daily production goals.
  2. Soil and Site Evaluation: Competent person classifies soil and inspects for cracks, water, surcharge loads, and adjacent structures.
  3. Excavation Layout: Establish dig lines, slope limits, spoils area, and equipment travel paths using flags or paint.
  4. Protective Systems: Install trench box or shoring prior to entering excavation; adjust as depth changes. Where sloping is used, maintain required angle (Type B soil: 1:1).
  5. Utility Exposure: Pothole and hand-dig within 18 inches of marked utilities. Stop and notify supervisor immediately if unmarked utilities are encountered.
  6. Entry and Work: Maintain ladder access every 25 feet, keep personnel clear of equipment swing radius, and prohibit working beneath suspended loads.
  7. Atmospheric Monitoring: Test for oxygen, combustible gases, and toxic vapors prior to entry when required; ventilate or evacuate if levels exceed action limits.
  8. Backfilling and Compaction: Place bedding material carefully around utilities, compact lift thickness per specification, and remove protective systems only when trench is safe to backfill.
  9. Daily Inspections: Competent person documents inspections at start of shift, after rain events, or after any hazard-increasing occurrence.
  10. Closeout: Backfill or install physical barriers if excavation left open; update as-built drawings and production logs.

6. Hazard Controls

  • Maintain spoil pile setback of at least 2 feet and use barricades when near public access.
  • Keep heavy equipment away from trench edges beyond the angle-of-repose distance.
  • Install trench shields to full depth; never stack mismatched boxes without engineering approval.
  • Inspect trenches after rain, vibration, or freezing conditions; do not allow entry until competent person signs off.
  • Restrict personnel in confined spaces when excavations reach 20 feet unless engineered shoring is installed.

7. Documentation and Weekly Acknowledgment

  • All crew members must acknowledge this SOP weekly via the personnel management app.
  • Supervisors verify acknowledgment status each Monday and coach any lapses prior to task assignment.
  • File inspection forms, utility locate tickets, and dewatering logs in the project folder daily.

8. References
