Underground Utility Installation Standard Operating Procedure

Weekly Acknowledgment: Required for crews installing water, sewer, storm, electrical, and communications conduits.

Scope: Applies to trench installation, pipe placement, manhole and structure setting, pressure testing, mandrel testing, and utility tie-ins for civil infrastructure projects.

1. Purpose

Provide a safe, high-quality method for installing underground utilities that protects existing services, controls groundwater, and meets alignment and leakage specifications.

2. Roles

  • Utility Foreman: Coordinates daily plan, ensures locates and permits, manages interface with inspectors.
  • Excavator Operator: Performs trenching per plan, supports shoring installation, and handles pipe with proper rigging.
  • Pipe Layer: Sets grade, installs pipe joints, checks alignment, and ensures gasket lubrication.
  • Topman or Spotter: Manages rigging, signals operator, and maintains spoil and traffic separation.
  • QC Technician: Verifies bedding, compaction, testing requirements, and records as-built data.

3. Pre-Installation Requirements

  1. Confirm utility locates and pothole all crossings using vacuum excavation or hand methods.
  2. Review plans, profiles, and standard details with crew; highlight grade tolerances, casing requirements, thrust blocking, and tracer wire layout.
  3. Verify trench boxes or shoring are rated for depth and soil; inspect lifting slings, spreader bars, and pipe hooks.
  4. Stage bedding materials, pipe, gaskets, lubricants, and testing equipment at laydown area.
  5. Ensure dewatering pumps, discharge hoses, and sediment controls are operational where groundwater is expected.

4. PPE and Tools

  • Standard PPE: hard hat, eye protection, high-visibility vest, gloves, steel-toe boots.
  • Confined space gear: gas monitor, tripod and retrieval system, ventilation blower when entering manholes.
  • Rigging: inspected chokers, pipe clamps, spreader bars, tag lines.
  • Grade control: laser level, grade rod, pipe laser, tape measures.

5. Installation Procedure

  1. Excavation: Cut trench to design width and grade, allowing space for shoring and bedding. Maintain slope or benching per excavation SOP.
  2. Bedding: Place and shape bedding material to required thickness; compact to specified density before pipe placement.
  3. Pipe Placement: Signal operator to lower pipe with tag lines; align spigot and bell; clean and lubricate gasket and spigot; push home to reference mark.
  4. Joint Inspection: Check gasket roll, witness marks, and interior alignment with feeler gauge or mirror.
  5. Haunching and Initial Backfill: Place select material evenly on both sides, compact in lifts to spring line, and avoid displacing pipe alignment.
  6. Structures: Set manholes or catch basins on solid foundation; install grade rings, barrel sections, and seals per manufacturer requirements.
  7. Testing: Conduct mandrel, low-pressure air, hydrostatic, or deflection tests as specified. Document results and corrective actions.
  8. Tracer Wire and Warning Tape: Install tracer wire with access boxes for non-metallic utilities; lay warning tape 12 inches above pipe.
  9. Final Backfill: Backfill and compact to surface grade; reinstall pavement or surface restoration per contract.

6. Hazard and Quality Controls

  • Inspect shoring and ladders daily; prohibit personnel in trench when boxes are being moved.
  • Use confined space entry permits for manhole work; maintain continuous gas monitoring.
  • Maintain proper separation from energized utilities; coordinate shutoffs with owners.
  • Verify bedding depth with grade rod every 25 feet; record invert elevations for as-built documentation.
  • Stop work and notify engineer if groundwater conditions change or unsuitable soils are encountered.

7. Documentation and Weekly Acknowledgment

  • Complete daily utility installation log (station, pipe size and type, invert elevations, compaction results).
  • Upload test reports, inspector sign-offs, and photographs to project management system.
  • Foreman verifies weekly SOP acknowledgments before assigning crew to utility trench work.
