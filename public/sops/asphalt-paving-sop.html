Asphalt Paving Operations Standard Operating Procedure

Weekly Acknowledgment: Required for paving foremen, paver and screed operators, roller operators, and quality control technicians.

Scope: Applies to placement of hot mix asphalt (HMA) and warm mix asphalt (WMA) for roads, parking lots, and airfields, including surface preparation, laydown, compaction, and quality control.

1. Purpose

Achieve consistent mat density, smoothness, and ride quality while protecting crew members from burn injuries, exposure to fumes, and traffic hazards.

2. Pre-Paving Coordination

  • Hold pre-pave meeting with plant, trucking, QC, and traffic control teams.
  • Confirm mix design, target temperature, tonnage, and delivery schedule.
  • Inspect base course, tack coat application, and drainage structures.
  • Review traffic control plan; deploy flaggers and signage before deliveries begin.

3. Equipment and Personnel Assignments

Position Primary Duties Safety Emphasis
Paving Foreman Coordinate laydown speed, monitor mat temperatures, communicate adjustments Ensure traffic control maintained, monitor crew fatigue
Paver Operator Maintain constant head of material, adjust augers, manage feeder conveyors Keep body parts away from conveyors and augers; lock out during cleaning
Screed Operator Control crown, depth, vibratory and tamping bars, match joints Use heat-resistant gloves; avoid stepping between paver and trucks
Roller Operators Perform breakdown, intermediate, and finish rolling as per rolling pattern Maintain safe operating speed, avoid sudden direction changes, use seatbelts
QC Technician Monitor mat and ambient temperatures, density readings, take cores if required Wear high-visibility gear and stay clear of reversing equipment

4. Required PPE

  • Class 3 high-visibility apparel for night or traffic-adjacent work.
  • Heat-resistant gloves, long sleeves, and pants to prevent burns from hot mix.
  • Safety glasses with side shields and face shield for tack and oil handling.
  • Hearing protection around paver, rollers, and compactors.
  • Respiratory protection as determined by air monitoring.

5. Paving Procedure

  1. Surface Preparation: Sweep base, repair soft spots, apply tack coat evenly. Ensure tack is cured before paving.
  2. Paver Setup: Preheat screed, set automation or guidance (ski, sonic, mobile string line). Verify screed plates are clean and level.
  3. Truck Exchange: Back trucks into paver using spotter; maintain slow, steady contact to prevent surge. Empty front hopper first.
  4. Laydown: Maintain consistent paving speed. Adjust feed to keep head of material covering augers half to three-quarters full.
  5. Joint Construction: Trim and tack cold edge; overlap new mat one to one and a half inches; set screed to match grade and slope.
  6. Compaction: Use rolling pattern with breakdown roller directly behind paver, followed by intermediate and finish rolling before mat temperatures drop below recommended limits.
  7. Quality Checks: Take thermal readings across mat, monitor density using nuclear gauge or intelligent compaction data, and adjust rolling pattern as needed.
  8. Cleanup: Remove excess asphalt from equipment, clean spray bars, inspect rollers, and secure work zone at end of shift.

6. Hazard Controls

  • Establish exclusion zone around the paver hopper and augers during operation.
  • Provide cooling stations, hydration, and heat illness monitoring during hot weather.
  • Use traffic spotters when working adjacent to live lanes; maintain lighting per MUTCD for night operations.
  • Inspect propane and diesel heaters for leaks at start-up; secure fuel cylinders upright.
  • Maintain good housekeeping to prevent slip or trip hazards from asphalt chunks or tools.

7. Documentation and Weekly Compliance

  • Record truck numbers, tonnage, start and stop times, temperatures, density results, and any corrective actions on the paving log.
  • Supervisors review weekly acknowledgment status each Monday and document in crew meeting minutes.
  • Retain QC reports, rolling pattern sheets, and plant tickets in the project quality binder.
